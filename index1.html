About me

The problem is greater than we imagine. Most people will be shocked to discover there are approx. 150 million orphans worldwide. According to UNICEF (The United Nations Children’s Emergency Fund), if orphans were a country of their own, the population would rank 9th in the world—ahead of Russia. Approximately 5 million Americans alive today are adoptees, 2-4 percent of all families have adopted, and 2.5 percent of all children under 18 are adopted but what about the rest? Families can expect to spend between $12,350 and nearly $14,000 a year, on average, to raise a child. With the amount of donation, although there are no global statistics on charitable giving, but the aggregate from United States, China, United Kingdom, Russia, Australia, and Switzerland is: $410.71 billion. Also, this is only talking about contributions and not nonprofit revenue, which can come from sale of products and services, and government funding. For comparison, total Nonprofit revenue in the US in 2015 exceeded $2 trillion. Even if we go by this basic numbers it is easy to help children lead a good life and make it through the tough phases of life. But still problems like Life as Refugees, Lack of Access to Education, Child Neglect, Child Labor, Child Prostitution, Internet Child Pornography, Trafficking and Slavery, Military Use of Children, Violence through Indoctrination, Malnutrition and poor health still exist on a globe level. We have come together to provide a solution and make these huge volume of money flow accountable and transparent and also provide a mechanism that will increase the chance of virtual adoption leading to better life of the future of the world, i.e. children
